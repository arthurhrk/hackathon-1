BLOCK $200 BILLION CREDIT MILESTONE

ANNOUNCEMENT:
- Date: January 20, 2026
- Block, Inc. announced it has provided access to more than $200 billion to customers in global lending across its portfolio of credit products

LENDING PRODUCTS COVERED:
1. Cash App Borrow — consumer lending within Cash App
2. Afterpay — buy now, pay later (BNPL) platform
3. Square Loans — merchant lending for Square sellers

SQUARE FINANCIAL SERVICES (SFS):
- Block's in-house bank
- Has originated more than $20 billion in loans across Square Loans and Cash App Borrow since inception
- In 2025, SFS began originating all Cash App Borrow loans (previously third-party originated)
- Expansion of in-house origination to reach more customers across the country

UNDERWRITING TECHNOLOGY:
- Enabled by Block's "novel underwriting technology and customer-first product designs"
- Maintaining stable loss rates across all lending products since 2013
- Stability maintained despite exponential growth and changing macroenvironments

CASH APP SCORE:
- Block completed a pilot providing select customers visibility into their Cash App Score
- Near real-time measure of financial health used for Cash App Borrow eligibility
- Results gave Block "confidence in expanding access within its lending suite"
- Exploring opportunities for Cash App Score to help customers qualify for external products (third-party lender use case)

STRATEGIC SIGNIFICANCE:
- Demonstrates scale of Block's lending ecosystem across consumer and merchant segments
- Shows execution on financial services deepening strategy
- In-house bank origination gives Block more control over credit operations
- Cash App Score as potential external product represents new revenue opportunity
- Positions Block as alternative to traditional credit systems

LENDING GROWTH CONTEXT (from Q4 2025 earnings):
- Consumer lending originations: +69% YoY in Q4 2025
- Borrow originations: +223% YoY in Q4 2025
- Full-year lending originations: +50% YoY
- Portfolio losses in Dec/Jan reflected mix shift to new borrower cohorts with "higher losses by design"
- Mid-February cohorts trending below loss targets
